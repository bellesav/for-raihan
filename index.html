Happy Birthday to my favorite person. 🤍

I've replayed the same songs hundreds of times, ordered the same food more times than I should admit, and gotten attached to a lot of random things. But somehow, you're still my favorite. More than every song on my playlist, more than my comfort food, and yes — even more than that nasi goreng from Kafe Maju Makmur that I keep ordering like it's part of my personality.

One thing you'll probably never understand is how proud I am of you. Not only when you achieve something big, but also in the little things. When you keep trying. When you choose to do what's right. When you stay kind even when nobody's watching. When you keep moving forward on days that don't go your way. I notice those things. Maybe more than you realize.

I don't think I've ever told you this enough, but I genuinely love watching you become the person you're meant to be. Every version of you has been my favorite so far, and I know the future versions will be too.

The funny thing is, some of my favorite memories with you aren't even the special ones. Not birthdays. Not anniversaries. Just random conversations, random moments, random days that somehow became important because they happened with you. Turns out, when it's you, even ordinary things feel worth remembering.

And can we talk about how terrible you are at hiding your emotions? Especially when you're upset. You can say you're fine all you want, but your face tells on you every single time. It's honestly one of my favorite things about you. Cute? A little. Funny? Definitely.

What I'll always remember most about you is how hard you try for the people you love. Especially for me. There are so many moments where you've gone out of your way just to make me happy, make me feel better, or make things easier for me. I don't know if you realize how much those things mean, but I carry every single one of them with me.

You're my safe place. My home. The first person I want to tell things to when something good happens. The first person I look for when life feels heavy. And somehow, even after all this time, you're still the person I want to share everything with.

So if anyone ever asks me what my favorite thing is, I could talk about music, food, movies, or a hundred other things. But the truth is, it's you. It's always been you.
